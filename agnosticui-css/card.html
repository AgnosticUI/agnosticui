Arbitrary Child Content Works Too!

Many frameworks allow you to pass in child markup or views. These are often called “slots” or “outlets”. This allows for a lot of flexibility and these child views can have their own styling applied. This test simply confirms that the general card frame looks good here.

Card Base / No Skin

Cards are split into two classes between base and skin: 

.card-base
and 
.card-skin
with 
.card
combining the two for convenience.

However, you can also choose to leave out the skin for flexibility. In this example, I've elected to use a 1 pixel border on the card itself, and not pull in the raised box shadow styles.
